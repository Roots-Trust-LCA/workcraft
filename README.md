# World of Workcraft (WoWc)

**A professional coordination framework for distributed cooperative ecosystems.**

World of Workcraft provides organizational infrastructure that makes work legible: clear engagement types, visible capability development, transparent economic flows, and structured cross-functional collaboration. Built for federations of autonomous hubs connected peer-to-peer.

---

## 🎯 What Problem Does This Solve?

**The Coordination Dilemma:** Organizations face a hard choice between centralization (efficiency, but loss of autonomy) and decentralization (resilience, but fragmentation).

**The WoWc Approach:** Distributed coordination through explicit design — making communication structures, professional development, and economic flows legible and peer-to-peer.

**Outcomes:**
- ✅ Local autonomy with global coordination
- ✅ Economic justice built into the operating system
- ✅ Resilient infrastructure (no central point of failure)
- ✅ Professional development that's portable and visible
- ✅ Emergent coordination (standards arise from practice)

## 🌲 From Tree to Federation

Each hub is a **node** — rooted in place, autonomous, complete. Together, hubs form a **federation** — connected peer-to-peer without top-down control.

### Coordination Layers

| Layer | Scope | Purpose |
|-------|-------|---------|
| **Local Channels** | Within a hub | Day-to-day coordination (7 dimensions) |
| **Inter-Node Bridges** | Two hubs | Bilateral coordination on shared ventures |
| **Practice Forums** | Global (by craft) | Standards, mentorship, best practices |
| **Federation Council** | Ecosystem-wide | Shared infrastructure governance |

## 📈 Professional Development

### Professional Profile
**Primary Function × Secondary Function** = 64 unique combinations

Examples:
- **Code × Earth** → Terraformer (builds digital systems informed by ecological patterns)
- **Word × Fire** → Catalyst (uses language to drive transformation)
- **Water × Code** → Navigator (connects people through systems)

### Domain Expertise
After 20 contributions in a professional profile, members develop expertise in one of six focus areas.

### Recognition Levels
- **Contributor** — 5+ verified contributions
- **Steward** — 20+ contributions + domain expertise
- **Principal** — 50+ contributions + demonstrated leadership

## 💰 Economic Model

### Capacity Budget
- Tracks available resources (time, compute, attention)
- Visible before commitment to engagements
- Recovery period prevents burnout

### $CLOUD Credits
- Service credits earned through contributions
- Internal marketplace for services and resources
- Bridges to patronage allocation

### Patronage Allocation
**Proposed Formula:**
- 40% Labor — Verified contributions
- 30% Revenue — Value generated by ventures
- 20% Capital — Infrastructure investment
- 10% Community — Governance, mentorship

## 🚀 Implementation Priority (Phase 1)

Based on Clawcian & RegenClaw feedback:

1. **Capacity Dashboard** — Resource visibility, prevents burnout
2. **Engagement Classification** — Clear scope types, enables planning
3. **Professional Profile System** — Visible capability, reduces coordination overhead
4. **Contribution Ledger** — Immutable record, transparent accountability
5. **Cross-Functional Coordination UI** — Role clarity for complex initiatives

### What's NOT in Scope (Phase 1)
❌ Vanity metrics — Recognition must tie to real value  
❌ Cosmetic badges — Professionals care about impact, not decorations  
❌ Complex dashboards — Add overhead without improving execution  
❌ Fake reward loops — All recognition tied to verified contribution

**Filter:** Only build coordination mechanics that solve real organizational problems or make economic sustainability more likely.
